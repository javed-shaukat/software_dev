About Pakistan

The Land of Contrasts

Pakistan is a country of remarkable diversity, from the soaring peaks of the Karakoram and Himalayan mountain ranges to the vast deserts of Sindh and Punjab. It's a land where ancient civilizations thrived, leaving behind a rich cultural heritage that continues to fascinate visitors.

The country is home to some of the world's highest mountains, including K2, the second-highest peak on Earth. Its northern areas are often called the "Paradise on Earth" for their breathtaking natural beauty.

Hunza Valley

The Hunza Valley is a mountainous valley in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan. It is known for its stunning scenery, including views of Rakaposhi (7,788 m), Ultar Sar (7,388 m), Bojahagur Duanasir II (7,329 m), Ghenta Peak (7,090 m), Hunza Peak (6,270 m), Darmyani Peak (6,090 m), and Bublimating (Ladyfinger Peak) (6,000 m).

The valley is also home to the ancient Baltit Fort and Altit Fort, which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The local people, known as Hunzakuts, are famous for their longevity and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit:

April to October offers the best weather for visiting Hunza Valley.

Lahore Fort

The Lahore Fort is a citadel in the city of Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. The fortress is located at the northern end of walled city Lahore, and spreads over an area greater than 20 hectares. It contains 21 notable monuments, some of which date to the era of Emperor Akbar.

The Lahore Fort is notable for having been almost entirely rebuilt in the 17th century, when the Mughal Empire was at the height of its splendor and opulence. The architecture of the fort reflects the architectural styles of the Mughals and the later Sikh and British occupants.

Key Features:
  • Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors)
  • Alamgiri Gate
  • Naulakha Pavilion
  • Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque)
Best Time to Visit:

From January to December offers the best weather for visiting Lahore City.

Fairy Meadows

Fairy Meadows is a grassland near one of the base camp sites of the Nanga Parbat, located in Diamer District, Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. At an altitude of about 3,300 meters above sea level, it serves as a launching point for trekkers summiting on the Rakhiot face of the Nanga Parbat.

The grassland is named by German climbers and is locally known as Joot. In 1995, the Government of Pakistan declared Fairy Meadows a National Park. The area is covered with dense pine forests and is one of the most beautiful places in Pakistan.

Activities:
  • Trekking to Nanga Parbat Base Camp
  • Camping under starry skies
  • Photography of breathtaking landscapes
  • Wildlife spotting
Best Time to Visit:

From March to November offers the best weather for visiting the Fairy Meadows.
